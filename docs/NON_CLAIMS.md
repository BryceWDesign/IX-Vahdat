# Non-Claims Statement

IX-Vahdat is a humanitarian water-resilience proof-of-concept. It is intended to support evidence gathering, conservative triage, review workflows, and planning for drought-stressed or infrastructure-stressed communities.

IX-Vahdat is not a certified water system.

## IX-Vahdat does not claim to provide potable water

The project does not certify any water as safe for drinking.

A `drinking_candidate` output means only that available evidence passed configured proof-of-concept triage checks and must still be reviewed by qualified humans under applicable local law, public-health requirements, laboratory testing requirements, treatment standards, and engineering controls.

The project does not replace:

- certified drinking-water treatment systems
- public-health approval
- laboratory analysis
- local permitting
- licensed engineering review
- trained field operators
- professional water-quality interpretation

## IX-Vahdat does not create guaranteed water supply

The project does not create water abundance.

Atmospheric water harvesting, fog capture, dew capture, water reuse, treatment, and emergency storage all depend on local conditions, hardware design, maintenance, energy availability, climate, humidity, source-water quality, and qualified review.

IX-Vahdat may help evaluate or route water-support options, but it does not guarantee:

- a minimum water yield
- a city-scale supply
- an agriculture-scale supply
- uninterrupted water availability
- safe drinking water
- regulatory compliance
- emergency-service adequacy

## IX-Vahdat does not authorize autonomous physical action

IX-Vahdat is not an autonomous physical-control system.

The project does not authorize automatic:

- water distribution
- public drinking-water release
- high-pressure pumping
- chemical dosing
- UV treatment approval
- excavation
- discharge
- recharge
- emergency allocation
- mechanical repair
- field construction

Any serious field action must be reviewed and approved by qualified humans with local authority.

## IX-Vahdat does not replace local law or public authority

Water safety, public health, environmental discharge, wastewater reuse, treatment, storage, construction, groundwater recharge, and emergency distribution are governed by local laws and competent authorities.

IX-Vahdat is not a permit substitute.

IX-Vahdat is not a public-health order.

IX-Vahdat is not a construction authorization.

IX-Vahdat is not a professional engineering stamp.

## IX-Vahdat does not guarantee fairness or prevent corruption

Evidence records and review bundles can improve accountability, but they do not guarantee institutional fairness, prevent all misuse, or prove intent.

The project can preserve decision evidence. It cannot replace ethical leadership, community consent, regulatory oversight, transparent governance, or lawful public administration.

## IX-Vahdat does not make country-specific political claims

IX-Vahdat is designed for drought-stressed and infrastructure-stressed communities in general.

The project does not prescribe policy to any nation, region, government, or community.

The project should be adapted only through lawful, local, technically qualified, and community-aware review.

## Safe summary

IX-Vahdat may help people by supporting:

- water-loss detection
- field telemetry
- water-quality evidence collection
- conservative use classification
- treatment-routing review
- emergency reserve planning
- infrastructure-health monitoring
- energy-readiness accounting
- maintenance blocking
- human-reviewed decision records

IX-Vahdat does not, by itself, make water safe, abundant, legal, certified, or automatically distributable.
