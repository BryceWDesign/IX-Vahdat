# Regulatory Notice

IX-Vahdat is a software proof-of-concept and reference architecture for humanitarian water-resilience planning.

It does not provide legal, regulatory, engineering, public-health, environmental, or construction approval.

## Local requirements control

Any real deployment must comply with applicable local requirements for:

- drinking-water treatment
- public-health testing
- wastewater reuse
- greywater reuse
- irrigation water use
- environmental discharge
- stormwater handling
- managed aquifer recharge
- groundwater protection
- construction
- electrical safety
- pressure systems
- pumps and storage tanks
- UV treatment equipment
- chemical treatment
- sensor calibration
- occupational safety
- emergency distribution
- community consent and access

## Qualified review required

The following reviewers may be required depending on location and use case:

- licensed civil engineer
- licensed environmental engineer
- licensed mechanical engineer
- licensed electrical engineer
- certified water-treatment operator
- public-health authority
- environmental regulator
- laboratory water-quality analyst
- groundwater or hydrogeology specialist
- local emergency-management authority
- community representatives
- legal or compliance advisor

## No permit substitute

IX-Vahdat outputs must not be submitted or interpreted as automatic proof of compliance.

Evidence bundles may support review, but they do not replace permits, inspections, certified tests, stamped plans, public-health approval, environmental approval, or lawful operating authorization.

## No drinking-water certification

A water-use classification from IX-Vahdat is not a drinking-water certification.

Any drinking-water use must be validated under applicable public-health requirements, with qualified testing, approved treatment, safe storage, and human authorization.

## No discharge authorization

A treatment-routing output from IX-Vahdat is not permission to discharge water to land, storm drains, rivers, canals, aquifers, wetlands, sewer systems, or public infrastructure.

Environmental discharge and recharge require applicable approval.

## No construction authorization

A site-readiness or infrastructure-health output from IX-Vahdat is not authorization to construct, excavate, modify, energize, pressurize, or operate physical infrastructure.

Qualified engineering and local authorization are required.

## No emergency authority

IX-Vahdat does not create emergency authority.

Any emergency allocation, rationing, public distribution, transport, or shelter operation must follow lawful local emergency-management procedures and human review.

## Use as review support only

Appropriate uses include:

- organizing evidence
- flagging missing data
- supporting conservative triage
- documenting review decisions
- comparing candidate water-support options
- preserving audit records
- identifying maintenance blockers
- identifying when qualified review is required

Inappropriate uses include:

- claiming water is safe without required testing
- bypassing local law
- bypassing public-health review
- bypassing permits
- authorizing autonomous water distribution
- authorizing autonomous physical action
- presenting proof-of-concept thresholds as legal standards
